Highlighted decisions March 2011

Great Britain and Northern Ireland decisions highlighted by the Editorial Board in March 2011

Capital

JRL v SSWP (JSA) [2011] UKUT 63 (AAC)
CJSA/3067/2009
Valuation of capital where terms and conditions allow bank to offset overdraft on one account against credit balance on another.

Carer’s allowance

SSWP v PW (CA) [2011] UKUT 3 (AAC)
CG/1154/2010
First-tier Tribunal was entitled to find non-degree course was part-time despite provider’s view to the contrary.

SSWP v ZC (CA) [2011] UKUT 2 (AAC)
CG/61/2009
Degree course with no formal exemption for previous qualification was full-time despite preparedness of student to work less than 21 hours per week.

Care standards

Ofsted v AF [2011] UKUT 72 (AAC)
HC/2014/2010
Registration of child care providers and waiver of disqualification.

Child support

AC v CMEC [2009] UKUT 152 (AAC)
CCS/1050/2009
Deprivation of income – paragraph 27 of Schedule 1 to the Child Support (Maintenance Assessment and Special Cases) Regulations 1992 applies whenever a parent intentionally acts to bring about a reduction in assessable income. For notional income, reducing the assessment must be an operative purpose but need not be the dominant purpose.

GR v CMEC (CSM) [2010] UKUT 436 (AAC)
CCS/1896/2009
Deprivation of income (paragraph 27 of Schedule 1 to MASC Regulations) – operative purpose is proper test not dominant purpose – deemed income from salary sacrifice to be treated as "other income" – no deduction for notional NI contributions – disregard of pension contributions does not apply to employers’ contributions.

SJ v CMEC (CSM) [2010] UKUT 355 (AAC)
CCS/1184/2010
Extra mortgage raised to buy out ex–partner – whether housing costs necessarily incurred.
To be reported as [2011] AACR 18.

Criminal injuries

CR v F-tT (SEC) & CICA (CIC) [2010] UKUT 388 (AAC)
JR/1127/2009
Guidance on applying paragraph 13(b) of the 2001 Scheme: "Failed to co– operate with the police ... in attempting to bring the assailants to justice".
To be reported as [2011] AACR 19.

MC v F–tT (SEC) & CICA (CIC) [2011] UKUT 87 (AAC)
JR/1363/2010
Breach of natural justice and fair procedure – Appeals Panel (3 members) made an order under paragraph 77 of the 2001 scheme that it could only make if the sole outstanding question was the amount of compensation.

R(CICA) v F–tT & IM (CIC) [2011] UKUT 70 (AAC)
JR/860/2010
2001 Scheme paragraph 35(d)(iii) – compensation for costs of care as consequence of the criminal injury.

Employment and support allowance

SC v SSWP (ESA) [2011] UKUT 48 (AAC)
CE/917/2010
Ability to use furniture in bending and kneeling (descriptors 3 (b) and (c)) is irrelevant but using the floor or a low shelf is relevant

Housing benefit / human rights

IB v Birmingham CC (HB) [2011] UKUT 23 (AAC)
CH/2823/2009
No unlawful discrimination in not providing higher LHA limit for disabled claimant needing room for carers.

Income

JK v SSWP (JSA) [2010] UKUT 437 (AAC)
CSJSA/411/2009
Money in bank account claimed to be held on trust for mother – proof of ownership required under Scots Law, rather than proof of trust.

Information rights

DEFRA v Information Commissioner [2011] UKUT 39 (AAC)
GIA/1694/2010 (DEFRA)
GIA/2098/2010 (Home Office)
Public body entitled to rely before Information Commissioner or First-tier Tribunal on exemption or exception not previously identified

Judicial review

Secretary of State for Justice v RB (JR) [2010] UKUT 454 (AAC)
HM/1533/2010
Upper Tribunal not bound by High Court decisions on JR where UT’s appellate role is the successor to the HC’s supervisory role – conditional discharge to care home under conditions amounting to detention is lawful.

Jurisdiction

LS v LB Lambeth (HB) [2010] UKUT 461 (AAC)
CH/1758/2009
JR/2204/2009
All decisions other than "excluded decisions" are appealable – paragraph 9 of Schedule 9 to HB Regs requires a decision to state why one category of non–dependant deduction applies rather than another, but a failure to include such detail does not prevent time for an appeal from running if the defect has caused no prejudice

Overpayments

WW v HMRC (CHB) [2011] UKUT 11 (AAC)
CF/395/2010
DWP officer had ostensible authority to make an oral representation that notification of a change of circumstances would be passed to HMRC – continuing duty to disclose so that fresh disclosure required after 3 months.

Retirement pension

AP v SSWP (RP) [2011] UKUT 64 (AAC)
CP/677/2010
Reciprocal agreements and pre 1975 national insurance credits

Special educational needs

Dudley MBC v Shurvington (SEN) [2011] UKUT 67 (AAC)
HS/800/2010
First-tier Tribunal entitled to find that additional travel costs were not disproportionate when giving effect to parental preference.

K v London Borough of Hillingdon (SEN) [2011] UKUT 71 (AAC)
HS/2846/2010
When a local authority or tribunal assesses costs involved in sending a child to a particular school, it must consider the wider social and health benefits when deciding whether additional public expenditure is unreasonable.

MS v LB Brent (SEN) [2011] UKUT 50 (AAC)
HS/1840/2010
Education at home with a 30% element of co-location in a school is education in a school – First–tier Tribunal, having decided that it was not inappropriate for provision to be made in a school, should have considered whether it was inappropriate for part of it to be made in a school.

Tax credits

MR v HMRC (TC) [2011] UKUT 40 (AAC)
CTC/1180/2009
Unjustified discrimination on nationality grounds in requiring claimant of 50+ element to have been entitled to UK benefit.

Tribunal procedure and practice

Ofsted v AF [2011] UKUT 72 (AAC)
HC/2014/2010
Discretion to admit late appeals.

SSWP v SS (DLA) [2010] UKUT 384 (AAC)
CDLA/892/2010
If a decision notice says that a decision was unanimous when it was by a majority, that is an error of law unless corrected e.g. in a statement of reasons, which must then give the minority reasons as well as the majority.
